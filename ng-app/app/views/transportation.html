By Air

Detroit Metropolitan International Airport (DTW) is the airport nearest to Ann Arbor (about 25 miles East of Ann Arbor). It serves as the major hub for airlines such as Delta, Spirit, U.S. Airways, etc.

From the airport, there are several options available to travel to University of Michigan, Ann Arbor.

By bus To get to University of Michigan from the airport, you can use AirRide (also called Michigan Flyer). The closest stop to UofM is Blake Transit Center (map).
  • Michigan Flyer : A one way ticket on Michigan Flyer can be purchased online at discounted rate of $12 and it is refundable until 48 hours prior to outbound departure. For unrestricted advanced reservation (fully refundable up to time of departure), phone reservation or cash walk-on fares, the rate of a one way ticket is $15. Book Here
By taxi / shuttle
  • Across Town Cab: 734.216.5932 – this is a van. Good for multiple passengers. Approximately $55-$65 one-way. Can be split among several passengers.
  • Metro Car: 800.456.1701 – Approximately $55-$65 one-way, non-inclusive of gratuity
  • Royal Limousine: 1.866.697.6925 – Approximately $55-$65 one-way, non-inclusive of gratuity.
  • Yellow Cab/Select Ride: 734.663.3355 – Yellow Cab also offers a shared shuttle for half the price. Please allow a half-hour window of when it will arrive for pick up.
  • Michigan Green Cab: 1.877.476.8294
  • Golden Limo: 1.800.300.5151 ext.1

* Prices may vary

By car (from DTW)

  2. Turn right (north) and continue on State Street approximately 2 miles to the main campus area.

By Car

Origin Directions
From DTW (heading West)
  1. Take I-94 West to the State Street exit (Exit 177).
  2. Turn right (north) and continue on State Street approximately 2 miles to the main campus area.
From Chicago (heading East)
  1. Take I-94 East to the State Street exit (Exit 177).
  2. Turn left (north) and continue on State Street approximately 2 miles to the main campus area.
From Ohio (heading North)
  1. Take US-23 North to the Washtenaw-"Ann Arbor" exit (Exit 37B) and merge onto Washtenaw.
  2. At the fork in the road where Stadium Blvd and Washtenaw split (approximately 2-3 miles), stay to the right on Washtenaw following the Hospital signs.
  3. Take a left at Hill Street (you'll see "The Rock"). Continue down Hill Street (campus buildings will be on your right).
  4. Take a right on State St. and continue two blocks to the central campus area.
From Northern MI (heading South)
  1. Take I-75 South to US-23 South to M-14 WEST.
  2. Take Exit #3, called Downtown Ann Arbor; this will become Main Street.
  3. Follow Main Street to William Street.
  4. Take a left at William Street and continue down William Street until it ends at State St. and the central campus area.
From Lansing
  1. Take I-96 East to US-23 South to M-14 WEST.
  2. Take Exit #3, called Downtown Ann Arbor; this will become Main Street.
  3. Follow Main Street to William Street.
  4. Take a left at William Street and continue down William Street until it ends at State St. and the central campus area.
From Northwest Suburbs of the Metro Detroit Area
  1. Take I-696 to I-275 South to M-14 West.
  2. Follow the M-14 West signs closely.
  3. Take Exit #3, called Downtown Ann Arbor; this will become Main Street.
  4. Follow Main Street to William Street.
  5. Take a left at William Street and continue down William Street until it ends at State St. and the central campus area.
Parts of Detroit, Redford, M-14, Plymouth and Canton
  1. Take I-96 to M-14 West.
  2. Follow the M-14 West signs closely.
  3. Take Exit #3, called Downtown Ann Arbor; this will become Main Street.
  4. Follow Main Street to William Street.
  5. Take a left at William Street and continue down William Street until it ends at State St. and the central campus area.
